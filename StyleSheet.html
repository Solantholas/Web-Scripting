All About Nebraska

I was born and raised in the simple state of Nebraska. It’s a simple farming state that is chock full of big-hearted people. It’s common for folks to hold the door open for you (even if you’re fifty feet away from the entrance). We wave to our neighbors and know them by a first name basis. And, if you’re lucky, you’ll find yourself stuck behind a tractor in a city of almost 300,000.

Nebraska prides itself as being a member of the Oregon trail. We have Chimney Rock, and the Platte River to name a couple landmarks off the top of my head. Just this year we had Bill Nye travel to Beatrice to witness the total solar eclipse at the Homestead National Monument. There is a lot of heritage in this state and it’s great to be able to visit these great landmarks in just a few hours.

If that isn’t enough, we have a fantastic selection of items straight from the farm. Nearly every city in Nebraska hosts a farmer’s market once a year. Usually, in the summer time, farmers from across the state will gather in the closest cities to sell their farm-fresh goods. Anything from canned pickles, to bars of soap are sold at these events.

Popular Haunts of Nebraska

  • The Henry Doorly Zoo in Omaha
  • The Air and Space Museum in Ashland
  • Chimney Rock just outside Bayard
  • The Haymarket District in Lincoln
  • The Homestead National Monument in Beatrice
